### Project Overview
This project analyzes healthcare patient data to identify key drivers of treatment cost, hospitalization duration, and patient risk patterns. The goal is to support better decision-making in healthcare delivery and resource allocation.
### Business Problem
Healthcare providers need to understand:

● What factors drive treatment costs

● Which patients are at higher health risk

● How to improve outcomes while managing resources efficiently

### Dataset Overview
● Records: 500 patients

● Features: 19 columns

● Time Period: Oct 2024 – Oct 2025

Key variables include:

● Demographics (Age, Gender)

● Health metrics (BMI, Blood Pressure)

● Conditions (Diabetes, Smoking Status)

● Financial (Treatment Cost)

● Outcomes (Recovered, Referred, Deceased)

### Tools Used
● Microsoft Excel (Data cleaning, Analysis and visualisation)

### Data Cleaning and Preparation 
● Standardized date formats

● Ensured unique patient records

● Created derived fields:
Age groups, Length of hospital stay, Risk indicators, Blood pressure categories

### Analytical Approach
●Segmented patients by age, condition, and risk level

●Focused on cost drivers, hospital stay duration, and outcomes

●Compared high-risk vs low-risk patient groups

### Key Questions
● Which conditions drive the highest costs?

● Do high-risk patients incur higher costs?

● Which age groups stay longer in hospitals?

● What factors influence patient outcomes?

### Key Insights
● Average treatment cost: ~$2,514 (high variability observed)

● 74% of patients are high-risk

● 82% have high blood pressure

● Average hospital stay: 7.3 days

● Majority of patients are aged 50+

● Chronic conditions (especially diabetes) are major drivers

### Recommendations
● Strengthen preventive healthcare programs

● Improve chronic disease management

● Optimize hospital resource allocation

● Investigate cost variability and standardize treatment processes

### Dashboard
The dashboard highlights:

●Patient demographics

●Cost distribution

●Hospital stay trends

●Risk segmentation

### Limitations
●Synthetic dataset (not real-world data)

●No treatment or medication details

●Limited clinical variables

### Conclusion
The analysis shows that chronic conditions and lifestyle risks significantly impact healthcare costs and hospital utilization.

Addressing these factors can improve patient outcomes while reducing operational strain on healthcare systems
